Gender Inequity in Financial Vulnerability

Across many countries, women remain disproportionately represented in vulnerable employment. This project explores structural patterns behind gender inequity in labor markets and its implications for financial institutions, policy makers, and economic systems. Data for this project has been sourced from the International Labour Organization (ILO).

Understanding the Key Concepts

Before exploring the visualizations, it is helpful to understand a few key terms used in this project. These concepts explain how employment vulnerability and gender inequities are measured across countries.

Vulnerable Employment

Vulnerable employment refers to jobs where workers have limited job security, unstable income, or limited access to benefits and legal protections.

Gender Employment Gap

The gender employment gap describes differences between men and women in employment conditions such as job stability, income security, and access to opportunities. The calculation used for this project is (Women - Male) = gender employment gap

Income Level Groups

Countries are grouped as low-, middle-, or high-income based on economic indicators such as GDP per capita.

How Vulnerable Employment Differs by Country Income Level

To understand gender inequity in financial systems, we first examine how vulnerable employment varies across global economies. This visualization compares employment vulnerability across low-, middle-, and high-income countries over time.

How this chart was calculated: This chart shows the average share of workers in vulnerable employment across countries grouped by income level. The values come from the International Labour Organization (ILO) modeled employment estimates from 1991-2023.

Global Gender Gaps in Vulnerable Employment

This visualization explores differences in vulnerable employment between men and women across countries around the world.

How this chart was calculated: Each country's vulnerable employment rate for men and women in 2023 was calculated using ILO modeled data. The gender gap represents the difference between the percentage of women and men working in vulnerable employment.

How Many Countries Experience High Vulnerable Employment

This chart helps show how widespread employment vulnerability is globally by counting how many countries fall into different employment categories in 2023. This chart was created using Altair.

How this chart was calculated: Countries were grouped based on their vulnerable employment rates. The chart counts how many countries fall into each category to illustrate the global distribution of employment vulnerability.

Country-Level Gender Disparities in 2023

This histogram shows the gender gap in vulnerable employment across countries in 2023. The chart can be filtered by region(s). Bars extending right indicate higher vulnerability among women, while bars extending left indicate higher vulnerability among men. The zero line represents zero gender gap which implies no gender based vulnerable employment.

How this chart was calculated: First calculation involved finding the percentage for men and women independently, and then taking the difference between the two (Women − Men).

Why These Patterns Matter for Financial Institutions

Gender disparities in vulnerable employment affect financial inclusion, credit access, and economic opportunity.

Access to Credit

Workers in vulnerable employment often lack stable income documentation.

Financial Product Design

Flexible savings tools and microfinance may better support irregular incomes.

Market Opportunities

Regions with high vulnerability may represent underserved financial markets.

Policy Collaboration

Financial institutions can partner with governments to expand inclusion.

Gender Inequity in Financial Vulnerability

Across many countries, women remain disproportionately represented in vulnerable employment. This project explores structural patterns behind gender inequity in labor markets and its implications for financial institutions, policy makers, and economic systems.

Understanding the Key Concepts

Before exploring the visualizations, it is helpful to understand a few key terms used in this project. These concepts explain how employment vulnerability and gender inequities are measured across countries.

Vulnerable Employment

Vulnerable employment refers to jobs where workers have limited job security, unstable income, or limited access to benefits and legal protections.

Gender Employment Gap

The gender employment gap describes differences between men and women in employment conditions such as job stability, income security, and access to opportunities.

Income Level Groups

Countries are grouped as low-, middle-, or high-income based on economic indicators such as GDP per capita.

Why These Patterns Matter for Financial Institutions

Gender disparities in vulnerable employment affect financial inclusion, credit access, and economic opportunity.

Access to Credit

Workers in vulnerable employment often lack stable income documentation.

Financial Product Design

Flexible savings tools and microfinance may better support irregular incomes.

Market Opportunities

Regions with high vulnerability may represent underserved financial markets.

Policy Collaboration

Financial institutions can partner with governments to expand inclusion.

Gender Inequity in Financial Vulnerability

Across many countries, women remain disproportionately represented in vulnerable employment. This project explores the structural patterns behind gender inequity in labor markets and its implications for financial institutions, policy makers, and economic systems.

Context Distribution Country Comparison Insights

Why Gender Inequity Matters

Financial inclusion and labor security are deeply connected. When women are overrepresented in vulnerable employment, they face greater income instability, reduced access to credit, and long-term economic precarity. Financial institutions play a critical role in recognizing and addressing these disparities.

How Wide Is the Gap?

This visualization shows the distribution of the gender gap across countries. Positive values indicate higher vulnerability among women, while negative values indicate higher vulnerability among men.

Country-Level Comparison

Looking at individual countries reveals where disparities are most pronounced. Sorting and filtering allow exploration of structural variation across regions.

What This Means for Financial Institutions

Persistent gender gaps in vulnerable employment signal systemic economic imbalance. Financial institutions can use this data to design more equitable credit systems, targeted financial products, and policy frameworks that address structural inequities.

⚠️ Data Note: The employment data used in this project is based on modeled ILO estimates and represents predicted values rather than raw survey-recorded data.
